Biography of Shannon Boldt:
I am an elder member of the Lummi Nation in Washington State. The Point No Point Treaty Council has employed me since 1995 as the Data Manager/Information Systems Manager. Since 2021, I have attended the Northwest Indian College studying Native Environmental Science, I will be graduating June 2026. With a main goal use environmental science to address disparities facing tribal communities. It was here at Northwest Indian College, that I was selected to attend an internship at Colorado University. After the internship ended, some of the group decided to create a working group to continue our research into using data to inform tribal communities of the state of their lands soil, air and water. We are going to look at contaminants left behind from legacy mining, water air, soil and flora. In November of 2024 our work group submitted a grant proposal to (ESIIL) Environmental Data Science Innovation & Inclusion Lab, working under the National Science Foundation, to work with NASA, and the University of Colorado. I am one of the Project Investigators for this grant; I am learning Python programming to be able to use the data from NASA. My goal is to contribute to this research project by utilizing the data we gather, then share with the two tribal research locations, focusing on environmental justice.
